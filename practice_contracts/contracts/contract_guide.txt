## 1. PolkaMeTypes — The Shared Language

Before anyone builds anything, the whole system needs a common vocabulary. This
contract is the DICTIONARY — it defines every enum, struct, and custom error
that the other four contracts speak.

The cast of characters it defines:

  IDENTITIES:
    - DIDDocument: your on-chain persona — a display name, reputation score
      0–100, timestamps.
    - DIDLink: the bridge that maps your EVM identity (did:ethr:0x...) to your
      Polkadot identity (did:polkadot:5Grw...).
    - Credential: proof of something — KYC, DAO badge, reputation, social
      proof, chain proof.
    - VerificationStatus: tracks email/governance/socials/kyc across three
      states: Unverified → Pending → Verified.

  ACCOUNTS:
    - LinkedChainAccount: Polkadot, Kusama, Astar, Moonbeam addresses.
    - LinkedSocialAccount: Twitter, Discord, Github handles.
    - AuthorizedDApp: apps you've granted access to.
    - ActivityEntry: an audit trail of what you did and where.

  GOVERNANCE:
    - Proposal: a referendum with aye/nay tallies and a deadline.
    - Validator: a node operator with commission & self-stake.
    - UserStake: your staking position — how much, your conviction multiplier
      1–6x, lock expiry, claimable rewards.

  SECURITY:
    - PrivacyPreference: toggles like "Stealth Mode".
    - Session: device/browser/location tracking.
    - SecurityLogEntry: timestamped security events.

  CUSTOM ERRORS:
    30+ gas-efficient revert errors (NotOwner, DIDRequired, TokensLocked, etc.)
    replacing verbose require strings — saving ~2KB of bytecode.

  INTERFACE:
    IPolkaMeIdentity — a thin interface so Accounts, Governance, and Security
    can call back into the Identity contract without importing all of its code.

Think of this as the CONSTITUTION — agreed-upon types before any law is written.

## 2. PolkaMeIdentity — The Foundation (Contract 1 of 4)

This is where your on-chain life BEGINS. Deployed first, it's the root of trust
for the whole system.

Story arc:

  1. A user arrives and calls createDID("Alice"). The contract mints a
     DIDDocument with a starting reputation of 10, initializes all four
     verification fields to Unverified, increments totalUsers, and emits
     DIDCreated. Alice now exists on-chain.

  2. Alice bridges worlds — linkPolkadotDID("did:polkadot:5Grw..."). She
     claims she owns a Polkadot address. A DIDLink is stored with
     verified: false. She's declared the link, but hasn't proven it yet.

  3. A trusted verifier confirms the link — verifyDIDLink(alice, 0). The
     verifier (set by the owner via setVerifier) flips verified to true and
     Alice earns +15 reputation. Her two worlds are now "Mapped Together."

  4. Credentials roll in. A verifier calls issueCredential(alice, KYC, ...) —
     Alice gets a KYC credential, her reputation jumps +20, and her kyc
     verification status flips straight to Verified. A DAO badge? +10 and
     governance goes to Verified. Social proof? +10 and socials verified.
     Each credential type has its own score weight, building a rich trust
     picture.

  5. The Verification Hub lets Alice manually submit verification requests per
     field (submitVerification(0) for email). It goes to Pending, a verifier
     later calls setVerificationState to approve or reject it.
     getVerificationProgress returns how far along she is — e.g., 2/3 steps
     done, 66% complete.

  6. Score delegation — this is the clever glue. The Accounts contract
     (deployed later) needs to award/deduct reputation when Alice links a
     chain account or verifies a social. But only Identity owns the score.
     So Identity exposes addScoreFor / subScoreFor, gated behind
     authorizedContracts. The owner whitelists the Accounts contract address,
     and now it can update Alice's score on her behalf.

  7. Platform stats (getPlatformStats) returns totals for the landing page:
     users, credentials issued, DID links created.

Identity is the PASSPORT OFFICE, CREDIT BUREAU, and REPUTATION ENGINE all
in one.

## 3. PolkaMeAccounts — The Web of Connections (Contract 2 of 4)

Deployed second, pointing at the Identity contract. This is where Alice FANS
OUT her identity into the wider ecosystem.

Story arc:

  1. Chain accounts — linkChainAccount(Polkadot, "My Relay", "5Grw...",
     "primary"). Alice proves she uses other chains. Each link gives her
     +5 reputation (delegated to Identity via addScoreFor). Removing one
     deducts -5. She can have as many as she wants — Polkadot, Kusama,
     Astar, Moonbeam, or Custom.

  2. Social accounts — linkSocialAccount(Twitter, "@alice_dot"). She claims a
     Twitter handle. It starts verified: false. A trusted verifier (maybe an
     oracle or a manual admin check) later calls verifySocialAccount(alice, 0),
     flipping it to verified and awarding +10 reputation. Removing a social
     costs -5.

  3. Authorized dApps — authorizeDApp("OpenGov Hub", 0xABC...). Alice grants
     a dApp permission to act on her behalf. The dApp address, name, and
     timestamp are stored. She can revokeDApp at any time — like revoking an
     OAuth token.

  4. Activity log — logActivity("Voted on Ref #801", "Governance", Success).
     Every meaningful action Alice takes is journaled on-chain. The frontend
     calls getRecentActivity to render a timeline.

Every function is gated behind onlyDIDOwner — you must have an Identity to use
Accounts. And every score change bounces back to Identity through the authorized
delegation system.

Accounts is the SOCIAL GRAPH and PERMISSION MANAGER.

## 4. PolkaMeGovernance — Power to the People (Contract 3 of 4)

Deployed third, also pointing at Identity. This is where reputation turns into
ACTION — staking, voting, and validator management, modeled after Polkadot's
conviction voting system.

Story arc:

  1. Alice stakes — stake{value: 1 ether}(). Her totalStaked goes up, and if
     she hasn't set a conviction yet, she defaults to 1x. Her votingPower =
     totalStaked × multiplier.

  2. Conviction — setConviction(3). Alice locks her tokens for 4 weeks
     (2^(3-1) = 4 weeks) in exchange for 3× voting power. Higher conviction
     = longer lock = more influence. This mirrors Polkadot's real conviction
     voting:

       Multiplier | Lock      | Voting Power
       -----------+-----------+-------------
       1x         | none      | stake × 1
       2x         | 2 weeks   | stake × 2
       3x         | 4 weeks   | stake × 3
       4x         | 8 weeks   | stake × 4
       5x         | 16 weeks  | stake × 5
       6x         | 32 weeks  | stake × 6

  3. A proposal is created — createProposal("Fund PolkaMe v2", "Build
     cross-chain...", 7 days). It gets auto-tagged as "Referendum #800"
     (incrementing), stored with ayeVotes: 0, nayVotes: 0, and a deadline.

  4. Alice votes — voteOnProposal(0, Aye). Her voting power (e.g., 3 ETH ×
     3x conviction = 9) is added to ayeVotes. She can only vote once. No
     stake? She still gets a weight of 1 — everyone has a voice.

  5. Time passes, voting ends. Anyone calls closeProposal(0). If ayeVotes >
     nayVotes, it passed. The proposal is deactivated.

  6. Rewards — the owner deposits ETH into the contract via depositRewardPool,
     then distributes to stakers via distributeReward(alice, amount). Alice
     claims with claimRewards, which transfers her claimableRewards balance.

  7. Validators — the owner registers node operators with their commission
     (in basis points — 300 = 3.0%), self-stake, and status (Active/Waiting/
     Inactive). The frontend pulls getValidators to display the validator set.

  8. Cross-chain credential sharing — shareCredentialToPolkadot(credIndex,
     linkIndex). This is the showpiece: Alice picks a credential and a
     verified DID link, and the contract emits CredentialShareRequested — an
     event designed to be picked up by an XCM relay or bridge, pushing her
     credential to the Polkadot side. It validates the credential isn't
     revoked and the link is verified and active before emitting.

Governance is the PARLIAMENT, TREASURY, and BRIDGE of the PolkaMe world.

## 5. PolkaMeSecurity — The Vault (Contract 4 of 4)

Deployed last, also pointing at Identity. This protects everything the other
contracts built.

Story arc:

  1. Privacy preferences — initializePrivacyPrefs(). On first call, Alice
     gets three default toggles:
       - Stealth Mode — hide balance on startup (default: ON)
       - Anonymous RPC — route through TOR/VPN (default: OFF)
       - Metadata Scrubbing — remove tx tags locally (default: ON)

     She can flip any toggle with updatePrivacyPreference(index, true/false).
     This only runs once per user (AlreadyInitialized guard).

  2. Session management — createSession("MacBook Pro", "Brave 1.62",
     "Berlin, DE", true). Every device Alice uses to interact with PolkaMe
     is registered. The isCurrent flag marks her primary device.

  3. Revoking sessions — revokeSession(2). If Alice sees a session she
     doesn't recognize, she kills it. The contract prevents revoking the
     current session (CannotRevokeCurrent error). Or she can go nuclear:
     revokeAllRemoteSessions() kills every session except the current one —
     a "log out everywhere" button.

  4. Security log — every revocation automatically logs an entry via
     _logSecurity. Alice can also manually log events with
     logSecurityEvent("Password Changed", "Settings Page"). The frontend
     pulls getSecurityLog to render an audit trail.

Security is the LOCKSCREEN, AUDIT TRAIL, and PANIC BUTTON.

## The Big Picture — Deploy Order & Architecture

The deploy order tells the whole story:

  1. IDENTITY goes first — it's the root of trust, the DID registry, the
     reputation engine.

  2. ACCOUNTS, GOVERNANCE, and SECURITY deploy next, each taking Identity's
     address in their constructor. They call back into Identity to check DIDs
     and update scores.

  3. The OWNER then calls setAuthorizedContract on Identity to whitelist the
     Accounts contract for score delegation.

Together, the four contracts form a DECENTRALIZED IDENTITY PLATFORM where a
user can:

  - Create a DID
  - Link it to Polkadot
  - Accumulate credentials
  - Connect social & chain accounts
  - Stake tokens
  - Vote on proposals with conviction
  - Share credentials cross-chain
  - Manage privacy
  - Track every security event

All anchored to a single reputation score from 0 to 100.
